Child Social Network Questionnaire

Mapping early social experiences to understand development

What CSNQ Measures

  • Network size – how many individuals a child regularly interacts with
  • Network composition – family member, caregivers, school teacher, peers, adults; diversity in language and race
  • Network structure – network density, racial and linguistic entropy, racial and linguistic EI Index

Why It Matters

Early social experience relates to neural, cognitive, and social development. Sensitivity to categories like race and language emerges early, and exposure to diversity can shape social cognition. Mapping children's social networks helps test how experience with diversity relates to outcomes like perspective-taking, bias, and emotion regulation.

Development Team

The Child Social Network Questionnaire (CSNQ) was developed in the Infant Learning and Development Laboratory (Woodward Lab) at the University of Chicago (woodwardlab.uchicago.edu). The initial CSNQ was created by Nicole Burke and colleagues (Burke, Brezack, & Woodward, 2022). Since then, Yiyi Wang, Bethany Ou, and collaborators have updated the survey and built this comprehensive research hub.
